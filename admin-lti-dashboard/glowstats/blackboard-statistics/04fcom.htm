In my experience, blackboard has been mostly used to post documents that were handed out in class, or media presentations used in conjunction with the class (including powerpoint presentations and overhead projections). Having those documents readily available online is useful if, say, one missed the class (which I would never do of course!), lost the document, or needed to see it when the hardcopy was not readily available (computers are everywhere!). I'd have to say that I still believe that blackboard is underutilized by many, but I this may be due to the fact that some of the blackboard features are as readily available, if not more so, in a student's own computer setup. Specifically (and this is all based on my own experience): Teachers don't use the announcement feature so much because an e-mail is just as easy and is checked more often than blackboard. On the other hand, if a fellow student in the class needed to get in touch with another person in that class or the whole class, blackboard allows them to do just that without having to harvest e-mail addresses. I've never seen a student actually do this, possibly for fear of other students becoming annoyed by such an act. The online scheduler is quite a fancy feature, and I would have immediately coordinated my schedule/planner with it, but my Microsoft Outlook already has my whole life in order, and I don't need to be on the network to have that available, and my planner is with me on the go, so there's really no advantage I can see. For courses that require online discussions/postings, blackboard is superior to e-mail correspondence. Nobody likes 30 or so e-mails over the span of a week, all pertaining to, say, naked mole-rats, clogging their inbox. If blackboard could organize those kinds of assignments into a better user interface, that would be great; I'm picturing some sort of virtual discussion board...and by that I mean something better than a long list of people's postings in the order they were received. So boring, and not very inviting to read. Few people actually post a response to another person's post, but I think that a better presentation of the discussion board interface would facilitate such postings. And while I absolutely love the idea of a virtual classroom setting ala Microsoft Netmeeting, i can't imagine many people would be open to the idea outside of a one-on-one, student-to-prof (or maybe one-or-two-student-to-prof) kind of setting. Submitting assignments through blackboard is not something I can say i've done (maybe once, but I can't even remember what or for which class it was) but I would think that a prof would also prefer that method over e-mail for the same reasons I would prefer blackbarod over the e-mailing of discussion posts. It's like spam....smart, academic spam, but spam nonetheless. I hope this helps! --Alejandro.
